# HN discussion: ACT-1 Transformer for Actions

Source: Hacker News item 32842860
Published around: 2022-09-14
Evidence: S3 community discussion / quality full

Useful feedback themes from the HN discussion:

- Reliability and repeatability were the dominant concern. One commenter asked whether ACT-1 could do data entry in Salesforce “roughly exactly the same thing every time” despite context/prompt variation.
- Adept-affiliated comments replied that they were thinking hard about reliability and saw human feedback plus post-processing model samples as important.
- Users asked about training data, benchmarks, human demonstrations, and whether the model was trained on example websites.
- Community concerns included irreversible database modifications, recovery plans, captcha/abuse, account trust, and natural language being too lossy for technical tasks.
- Positive comments framed ACT-1 as a major step toward action-oriented computing and “actionable Google search.”

Research value: the core questions around agent reliability, permission, recovery, abuse, repeatability and language precision were already visible in 2022. These same questions apply to [[company.viktor]] and modern enterprise agents.
