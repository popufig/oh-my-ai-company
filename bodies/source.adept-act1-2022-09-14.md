# ACT-1: Transformer for Actions

Source: https://www.adept.ai/blog/act-1/
Published: 2022-09-14
Evidence: S1 official / quality full

Adept introduced ACT-1 as a large-scale Transformer trained to use digital tools. It was connected to a Chrome extension that observes browser state and takes actions like clicking, typing, and scrolling.

Key product facts:

- Adept’s framing of general intelligence: a system that can do anything a human can do in front of a computer.
- ACT-1 uses a custom rendering of the browser viewport and an action space based on UI elements available on page.
- Demos included high-level requests, Salesforce workflows, spreadsheets, multi-tool tasks, online lookup, and correction through human feedback.
- Adept emphasized human feedback and staged deployment to reduce misuse and misalignment.

Research value: ACT-1 is one of the earliest high-profile “computer-use/action model” examples, directly relevant to [[company.viktor]] and browser/enterprise agent products.
