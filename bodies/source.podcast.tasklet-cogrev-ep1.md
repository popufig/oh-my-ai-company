Key claims:
- Origin: Shortwave users connected AI to Notion/Asana/HubSpot via MCP and asked for automatic morning runs; team realized the agent itself was product, not email UI.
- Agent-first vs workflow-first: Tasklet puts model/agent in control; traditional software/API/code are tools. Workflow products put software flowcharts in control and call LLMs inside nodes.
- Architecture: connections, triggers and team collaboration/oversight as major product layers.
- Andrew’s thesis: always bet on models; speed is the only moat; vertical SaaS and UI-heavy products face pressure from dynamic AI-generated UI.
- He avoided “virtual employee” marketing even though long-term direction fits it.
