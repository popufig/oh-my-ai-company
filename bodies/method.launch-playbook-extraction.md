# Method: Extract launch playbooks from company self-posts

Status: draft. This came from Paid's unusually detailed launch playbook and should be tested on more companies before becoming SOP.

When a company publishes a launch/fundraising/playbook post, don't only summarize the announcement. Extract it as a GTM artifact:

1. Timeline
- What date did they choose as the public launch?
- What assets were prepared before launch?
- What was the working-backward schedule?

2. Narrative
- One-line story.
- Villain / urgency / why-now.
- Product category they try to own.
- Phrases they repeat across channels.

3. Channels
- PR/news outlets.
- Founder social posts.
- Investor amplification.
- Podcast/video.
- Launch party/event.
- Community/product channels.

4. Assets
- Website, manifesto, video, images, banners, screenshots, demo, founder talking points.

5. Metrics
- Visitors/views.
- Followers/impressions/reactions/reposts.
- Signups/meetings.
- Customer/investor attendance.
- Time-to-capacity bottlenecks.

6. Tradeoffs
- What they deprioritized.
- What broke on launch day.
- What they would do differently.

7. Founder-network adjustment
- If founder has prior reputation, record it as part of distribution advantage. Do not attribute all launch performance to channel tactics alone.
