# Human data supply layer

As frontier AI moves from text/web corpora to robotics, speech, video, computer-use, embodied interaction, and world models, the bottleneck shifts toward rights-cleared, high-signal, multimodal human data.

[[company.luel]] is the concrete case in this vault:
- It describes itself as a rights-cleared data marketplace plus collection engine.
- Buyers can request samples, license existing datasets, or brief bespoke collections.
- The public catalog exposes audio, video, and sensor datasets, including computer-use workflows, egocentric video, multilingual speech, music, and healthcare conversations.
- Contributors perform paid collection tasks; suppliers can apply to list private datasets.
- Marketplace trust is built around rights clearance, provenance tracking, and quality audit.

Why this is part of the broader [[concept.agent-substrate-stack]]:
- Agents and world-model systems need fresh, task-specific, legally usable data.
- Retrieval, orchestration, monetization, and security are downstream of having data/models that can perform in real-world contexts.
- The same pattern creates operational risk: the supply side is human labor and third-party data ownership, so payout, trust, QA, rejection fairness, and rights posture are product risks.

Risk evidence: [[source.reddit.luel-wfhjobs-complaint-2026-07-08]] is S3 single-party evidence and should not be treated as settled fact, but it points to a real category of risk for any human-data marketplace.
